AutoHotKey

AutoHotKey is a program that the surgical pathology residents and attendings use for quick-texts. It auto-populates things you write a lot, and can save time. Either get one of them to send it to you and show you how to set it up, set it up from my guide, or set it up from scratch. Unless you got it from another resident, then the specific molecular templates we use won't be in there. They are listed below. Just copy them into the Molecular section of your .ahk file.

  • ;Myeloseq
  • :*:mclin\::[xx]-year-old [xx] with a history of [xx] (diagnosed YYYY-MM-DD) with [cytogenetic abnormalities], who is status post hematopoietic cell transplant (YYYY-MM-DD) and presents for [evaluation|treatment|further workup]. Corresponding bone marrow biopsy (SXX-XXXXX) shows [xx]. Corresponding cytogenetic studies (GXX-XXXXX) show [list of FISH findings]. Corresponding ChromoSeq study (WXX-XXXXX) showed [list of variants].
  • :*:mnew\::Mutations in [xx] are identified. This case is best classified as [xx] by ICC criteria or [xx] by the 5th edition World Health Organization Classification of Haematolymphoid Tumours (WHO-HAEM5). The overall constellation of mutations is consistent with [xx] by National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) guidelines. Overall quality control metrics are within normal limits.
  • :*:mrep\::Previously identified mutations in [xx] are again seen, consistent with persistent molecular disease (measurable residual disease (MRD) positivity). Additional new mutations in [xx] are identified. The previously identified mutations in [xx] are not seen. [chimerism] Overall quality control metrics are within normal limits.
  • :*:mnewneg\::No mutations are identified. Overall quality control metrics are within normal limits. The absence of a mutation detectable with this assay does not rule out hematopoietic malignancy.
  • :*:mneg\::Previously identified mutations in [xx] are not seen, consistent with mutation clearance and measurable residual disease (MRD) negative status at a sensitivity of at least 0.1%. [chimerism] Overall quality control metrics are within normal limits.

Managing the Sendout Log

New cases appear in the ‘‘Pending’’ tab. You should click them and hit [update] button to add them to your queue. Your queue is in the ‘‘My Worklist’’ tab. Organize your worklist by sorting the cases by ‘‘Id.’’ You should address new cases 2-3 times per day and run the entire Worklist daily to check for updates. Before closing a case, if there are relevant biopsies or cytogenetics/FISH in CoPath, the attendings like these results recorded in the log. Tests that were already auto-approved do not bear thinking about, just hit the ‘‘approve’’ button and move on. It is normal for cases to drag out for several days awaiting bone marrow and FISH/cytogenetic results.

Cases will fall into three categories: Hematologic molecular algorithm (HMA), hematologic non-HMA, and non-hematologic.

  • HMA cases are characterized by an HMA order. follow the instructions in the HMA for them.

  • Hematologic non-HMA cases are characterized by hematologic orders such as FLT3 or Myeloseq, but no HMA order. For these you should approve if indicated by the HMA, or if not indicated by the HMA, ask the clinicians what they’re looking for and whether they’d like to change to testing indicated by the HMA. If there is no HMA order, you cannot add additional testing, so if the clinician wants it, they must order it.

  • Non-hematologic cases involve miscellaneous sendout tests. These are similar to test approvals from chemistry and micro, but for the fact that we rarely say ‘‘no.’’ Information on what you should do with these may be found herein (Molecular Sendout Testing (alphabetical order)) or if you search historical cases in the call log.

DPYD Testing

DYPD Genotyping Testing laboratory: MAYO
Test ID: DPYDQ
Test Webpage: Link
Indication: Variants in DPYD may cause impaired metabolization of 5-fluorourocil (5-FU) or capecitabine. This test detects eight common variants that cause reduced DPYD activity. It is appropriate if clinicians are planning to treat with 5-FU or capecitabine and want to determine proper dosage.

DYPD Full Gene Sequencing Testing laboratory: MAYO
Test ID: DPYDG
Test Webpage: Link
Indication: Variants in DPYD may cause impaired metabolization of 5-fluorourocil (5-FU) or capecitabine. It is most likely if this test is ordered that the clinicians actually want the above, DPYD genotyping test (DPYDQ). Compared to that test, this one detects a broader range of potential variants. It is appropriate if clinicians have a high suspicion for an atypical variant of DPYD variation that would not otherwise be detected by the DPYDQ test and are trying to determine the appropriate dosage of 5-FU or capecitabine.

KIT Asp816Val Mutation Analysis

Testing laboratory: MAYO
Test ID: KITVS
Test Webpage: Link
Indication: This test detects the most common mutation in systemic mastocytosis. It can be subject to false negative in peripheral blood specimens if there are too few circulating mast cells (<0.1%). The test may be indicated in patients for which there is clinical concern for systemic mastocytosis, such as those with otherwise unexplained hives, flushing, anaphylaxis, diarrhea, and lymphadenopathy. There is a low threshold for approval.

Telomere Length Measurement

Testing laboratory: RepeatDx (Vancouver)
Test ID: N/A
Test Webpage: Link
Indication: Specimen must be recieved by reference lab within 48-hours of collection, by extension it must be received in our lab on or before Wednesday. Indicated for dyskeratosis congenita, idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, and possibly other genetic disease. The 2-panel test is based on just lymphocyte/granulocytes while the 6-panel test splits lymphocytes into further subsets. There is no clinical reason to do the 6-panel method, and all clinical guidelines are based on the 2-panel method. Johns Hopkins also performs this testing, and we may begin sending this to them in future to avoid delay in Customs.

Setup Myeloreporter

  1. Go to \\pathfiles.wustl.edu/amp/Genomics/Myeloseq_Results/Myeloreporter
  2. Copy index.html into your browser. Bookmark this link so you can access it quickly in future.

Interpret Myeloseq
  1. Open Myeloreporter. You should have it bookmarked.
  2. Click [Choose File]
  3. Open \\pathfiles.wustl.edu/amp/Genomics/Myeloseq_Results/[current batch]/TWAO-[MRN]-[Accession number]-[Specimen type]-lib1.json
  4. Check that the testing is valid: At the bottom, make sure that meanCoverage is >1000.00 and contaminationEstimate is <0.0700
  5. Fill out the Variant Interpretation, Clinical History, and Specimen Type fields per Dr. Hughes' guide. AutoHotkey can improve your efficiency.
  6. Click Generate and download RTF. Repeat the above for all your cases before moving onto the next steps.
  7. Open the .rtf file in Microsoft Word and correct any formating errors. Make sure there appropriate spaces after gene names.
  8. Open CoPath, navigate to the Procedure/Addendum tab, and open the case.
  9. Change the procedure pathologists to the assigned attending and yourself.
  10. Click [Edit text] and paste-with-formating (Ctrl+Shift+V) the information from Microsoft Word into the appropriate fields in CoPath.
  11. Save by clicking the purple box button then Complete/Send to Pathologist.
  12. Repeat for all cases.
